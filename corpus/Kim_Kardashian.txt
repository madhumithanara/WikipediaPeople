'''Kimberly Noel Kardashian West'''<!--DO NOT CHANGE THE NAME!--><ref>{{cite web |last1=Nessif |first1=Bruna |last2=Baker |first2=Ken |title=Kim Kardashian Reveals She's Taking Kanye West's Last Name |url=https://www.eonline.com/news/475104/kim-kardashian-reveals-she-s-taking-kanye-west-s-last-name-watch-now|website=[[E! Online]] |accessdate=June 25, 2019 |date=October 28, 2013}}</ref> (''[[née]]'' '''Kardashian'''; born October 21, 1980) is an American media personality, socialite, model, businesswoman, and actress. Kardashian first gained media attention as a friend and [[personal stylist|stylist]] of [[Paris Hilton]] but received wider notice after a 2002 [[celebrity sex tape|sex tape]], ''[[Kim Kardashian, Superstar]]'', with her then-boyfriend [[Ray J]] was released in 2007.<ref>{{cite web |title=The Kim Kardashian Sex Tape An Oral History |url=https://pagesix.com/2017/03/27/the-kim-kardashian-sex-tape-an-oral-history/ |publisher=Page Six}}</ref> Later that year, she and [[Kardashian|her family]] began to appear in the [[E!]] reality television series ''[[Keeping Up with the Kardashians]]'' (2007–present). Its success soon led to the creation of [[Spin-off (media)|spin-off]]s including ''[[Kourtney and Kim Take New York]]'' (2011–2012) and ''[[Kourtney and Kim Take Miami]]'' (2009–2013).

Kardashian has developed an online and [[social media]] presence, including hundreds of millions of followers on [[Twitter]] and [[Instagram]].<ref name="guard"/><ref name="et">{{cite web|last1=Seemayer|first1=Zach|title=Kim Kardashian on the Success of Her Mobile App and Her Social Media Empire|url=http://www.etonline.com/news/160473_kim_kardashian_on_the_success_of_her_mobile_app_and_her_social_media_empire/|website=[[ET Online]]|accessdate=June 11, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://socialblade.com/instagram/top/100/followers|title=Top 100 Instagram Users by Followers}}</ref> She has released a variety of products tied to her name, including the 2014 [[mobile game]] ''[[Kim Kardashian: Hollywood]]'', a variety of clothing and products, the 2015 photo book ''[[Selfish (book)|Selfish]]'' and her eponymous personal app. Her relationship with rapper [[Kanye West]] has also received significant media coverage; the couple married in 2014 and have four children together.<ref name="auto">Caramanica, Jon. [https://www.nytimes.com/2015/04/10/t-magazine/kanye-west-adidas-yeezy-fashion-interview.html?_r=0 "The Agony and the Ecstasy of Kanye West."] ''[[New York Times]]''. April 10, 2015.</ref> As an actress, Kardashian has appeared in films, such as ''[[Disaster Movie]]'' (2008), ''[[Deep in the Valley]]'' (2009), and ''[[Temptation: Confessions of a Marriage Counselor]]'' (2013).

In recent years, Kardashian has focused on her own businesses by founding KKW Beauty and KKW fragrance in 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://stylecaster.com/kim-kardashian-kkw-beauty-career-timeline/|title=Kim Kardashian’s Business Glow-Up Has Been Hella Remarkable|last=Tinubu|first=Aramide|date=October 21, 2019|website=StyleCaster |language=en|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> In 2019 she launched Skims which was previously called [[Kimono]] but the name was changed due to immediate backlash.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.glamour.com/story/skims-kim-kardashians-solutionwear-line|title=Everything We Know About Skims, Kim Kardashian's Solutionwear Line|last=Marinelli|first=Gina|website=www.glamour.com|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> She has also become more politically active by lobbying president [[Donald Trump]] for prison reform and lobbying for [[Alice Marie Johnson]] to be granted clemency.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/arts-entertainment/2019/01/31/kim-kardashian-is-still-fighting-criminal-justice-reform/|title=Kim Kardashian is still fighting for criminal justice reform|last=Helena Andrews-Dyer|website=Washington Post|language=en|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> Kardashian is also planning to become a lawyer by doing a four-year law apprenticeship that is supervised by the law firm #cut50 at which [[Van Jones]] is a partner.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/sarabliss/2019/04/18/kim-kardashian-is-becoming-a-lawyer-what-her-move-can-teach-you-about-making-a-career-leap/|title=Kim Kardashian Is Becoming A Lawyer: What Her Move Can Teach You About Making A Career Leap|last=Bliss|first=Sara|website=Forbes|language=en|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vogue.com/article/kim-kardashian-west-cover-may-2019|title=The Awakening of Kim Kardashian West|last=Meter|first=Jonathan Van|website=Vogue|language=en|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref>

''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' magazine included Kardashian on their list of 2015's [[Time 100|100 most influential people]]. Both critics and admirers have described her as exemplifying the notion of being [[famous for being famous]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.scoreboard-media.com/paris-hilton-rule/ |title=The Paris Hilton Rule: Famous For Being Famous |work=Scoreboard Media Group |accessdate=September 13, 2014 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120528065137/http://www.scoreboard-media.com/paris-hilton-rule/ |archivedate=May 28, 2012 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wetpaint.com/sure-shes-beautiful-but-why-is-kim-kardashian-famous-697080/|work=Wet Paint|title=Sure, She's Beautiful, but Why is Kim Kardashian Famous?|date=November 9, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.debate.org/opinions/does-kim-kardashian-deserve-to-be-famous|work=Debate|title=Does Kim Kardashian deserve to be famous?}}</ref> She was reported to be the highest-paid reality television personality of 2015, with her estimated total earnings exceeding US$53 million.<ref>[[Forbes]] [https://www.forbes.com/profile/kim-kardashian/ "Kim Kardashian"] Retrieved on December 6, 2015.</ref>

==Career==
=== Beginnings (2003–2006) ===
Kardashian got her first stint in show-business as friend and stylist of [[Paris Hilton]], appearing as guest on various episodes of the Hilton-starring reality television series ''[[The Simple Life]]'', between 2003 and 2006.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.glamourmagazine.co.uk/article/paris-hilton-interview-2015-simple-life-dj-and-perfume|title=EXCLUSIVE: Paris Hilton tells us where she's been hiding|last=Glamour|website=Glamour UK|access-date=August 18, 2019}}</ref>

===Reception to product range ===
In June 2019, Kardashian launched a new range of shape wear called Kimono. Kardashian was heavily criticised over the name of the brand which critics argued disrespected Japanese culture and ignored the significance behind the [[Kimono|traditional outfit]]. Following the launch of the range, the hashtag #KimOhNo began trending on [[Twitter]] and the mayor of [[Kyoto]] wrote to Kardashian to ask her to reconsider the trademark on Kimono. In response to public pressure, in July 2019, Kardashian announced that she would change the name.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2019/jul/01/our-traditional-garment-is-not-spandex-kim-kardashian-west-renames-brand-amid-outcry|title=#KimOhNo: Kim Kardashian West renames Kimono brand amid outcry|last=Ho|first=Vivian|date=July 1, 2019|work=The Guardian|access-date=July 2, 2019|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> However, Japanese trade minister [[Hiroshige Sekō|Hiroshige Seko]] stated that he would still be dispatching patent officials for a meeting at the [[United States Patent and Trademark Office|U.S. Patent and Trademark Office]], and that [[Japan]] would keep an eye on the situation.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnn.com/style/article/kim-kardashian-rename-kimono-intl-scli/index.html|title=Japan to send trademark officials to US over Kim Kardashian West 'kimono' row|last=Woodyatt|first=Amy|date=July 1, 2019|website=CNN Style|language=en|access-date=July 2, 2019}}</ref> She later replaced the shapewear company to the name Skims.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2019/08/26/kim-kardashian-names-shapewear-company-skims-plans-september-launch.html|title=In wake of Kimono uproar, Kim Kardashian names shapewear company Skims|website=CNBC}}</ref>

===Paris robbery===
On October 2, 2016, while attending [[Paris Fashion Week]], Kardashian was [[Robbery|robbed at gunpoint]] in the apartment where she was staying. Five individuals, dressed as police officers, bound and gagged her, then stole $10 million worth of jewelry.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.teenvogue.com/story/kim-kardashian-bound-gagged-at-gunpoint | title= Kim Kardashian Bound and Gagged at gunpoint | work= Teen Vogue | accessdate=October 4, 2016}}</ref> The thieves got in her residence by threatening the concierge. Once they accessed Kardashian's room, they held a gun to her head, tying her wrists and legs and wrapping duct tape around her mouth as a [[gag]].<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.nydailynews.com/news/national/kim-kardashian-bound-begging-life-paris-robbery-article-1.281555 | title = Kim Kardashian was bound, gagged and begging for life during Paris jewelry robbery | work = New York Daily News | accessdate=October 4, 2016}}</ref> Kardashian, who was placed in the bathtub, was physically unharmed and reportedly begged for her life.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/people/2016/10/03/kim-kardashian-paris-trauma-what-we-know-kanye-west/91466398/ | title=Kim Kardashian's Paris trauma: What we know | work=USA Today | date=October 3, 2016 | accessdate=October 3, 2016 | last=Puente |first=Maria}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | last=Charlton | first=Angela | last2=Petrequin | first2=Samuel | title=Armed jewelry thieves target Kardashian West in Paris | website=The Big Story | date=October 3, 2016 | url=http://bigstory.ap.org/article/ca4cd8a750184f85891d9905c53809f2/spokeswoman-kardashian-west-held-gunpoint-paris | accessdate=October 4, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | last=Grinberg | first=Emanuella | last2=Melas | first2=Chloe | last3=Sutton | first3=Joe | title=Kim Kardashian West in New York after robbery | website=CNN | date=October 3, 2016 | url=http://www.cnn.com/2016/10/02/entertainment/kanye-west-family-emergency/index.html | accessdate=October 4, 2016}}</ref> She managed to wriggle her hands free from the plastic ties around her wrists and scream for help.
