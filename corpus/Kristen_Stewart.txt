'''Kristen Jaymes Stewart''' (born April 9, 1990) is an American actress and filmmaker. The world's highest-paid actress in 2010 and 2012, she is the recipient of [[List of awards and nominations received by Kristen Stewart|several accolades]], including a [[BAFTA Award]] and a [[César Award]], which she was the first American actress to win.

Born and raised in [[Los Angeles]] to an American father and Australian mother, both of whom work in show business, Stewart first gained notice at age 12 for her role as the daughter of [[Jodie Foster]]'s character in [[David Fincher]]'s thriller ''[[Panic Room]]'' (2002), which earned her a [[Young Artist Award for Best Leading Young Actress in a Feature Film|Young Artist Award]] nomination for [[Young Artist Award for Best Leading Young Actress in a Feature Film|Best Leading Actress in a Feature Film]]. She subsequently starred in ''[[Speak (film)|Speak]]'' (2004), ''[[Catch That Kid]]'' (2004), ''[[Zathura: A Space Adventure]]'' (2005), and ''[[Into the Wild (film)|Into the Wild]]'' (2007). In 2010, she was awarded the [[BAFTA Rising Star Award]]. Stewart went on to gain worldwide recognition for her role as Bella Swan in ''[[The Twilight Saga (film series)|The Twilight Saga]]'' film series (2008–2012), which collectively grossed over $3.3 billion worldwide.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.boxofficemojo.com/franchises/chart/?id=twilight.htm|title=Twilight Movies at the Box Office - Box Office Mojo|website=boxofficemojo.com|access-date=June 3, 2016}}</ref>

After starring in the fantasy film ''[[Snow White and the Huntsman]]'' (2012), Stewart eschewed roles in big-budget films in favor of [[Independent film|independent productions]] for the next few years. She took on roles in the dramas ''[[Camp X-Ray (film)|Camp X-Ray]]'' and ''[[Still Alice]]'' (both 2014), and the science fiction romance ''[[Equals (film)|Equals]]'' (2016). In 2015, she garnered critical acclaim for her performance in [[Olivier Assayas]]' drama film ''[[Clouds of Sils Maria]]'', which won her the [[César Award for Best Supporting Actress]]. Stewart reunited with Assayas the following year in the supernatural thriller ''[[Personal Shopper]]'' (2016) and made her directorial debut with the 2017 short film ''[[Come Swim]]''. She returned to mainstream Hollywood with leading roles in the action films ''[[Charlie's Angels (2019 film)|Charlie's Angels]]'' (2019) and ''[[Underwater (film)|Underwater]]'' (2020).

==Career==
===1999–2003: Career beginnings===
As she was raised in a family who work in the entertainment industry as non-actors, Stewart thought that she would become a screenwriter/director, but never considered being an actor. She recalled, "I never wanted to be the center of attention—I wasn't that 'I want to be famous, I want to be an actor' kid. I never sought out acting, but I always practiced my autograph because I love pens. I'd write my name on everything."<ref name="Biography Today">{{cite book |title=Biography Today |year=2010 |page=[https://archive.org/details/biographytodayge0000unse_h7i3/page/156 156] |publisher=Omnigraphics |location=Detroit |isbn=978-0-7808-1058-7 |url-access=registration |url=https://archive.org/details/biographytodayge0000unse_h7i3/page/156 }}</ref>

She began acting at age eight, after an agent saw her perform in her elementary school's Christmas play.<ref>{{cite web | title=Girls of Fall: Autumn's Crop of Enchanting Entertainers | url=http://www.wwd.com/lifestyle-news/girls-of-fall-autumns-crop-of-enchanting-entertainers-1803246#/article/lifestyle-news/girls-of-fall-autumns-crop-of-enchanting-entertainers-1803246?page=8 | work=[[Women's Wear Daily|WWD]] | date=September 29, 2008 | accessdate=November 4, 2008}}</ref> After auditioning for a year, Stewart got her first role with a small, nonspeaking part in the [[Disney Channel]] television film ''[[The Thirteenth Year]]''.<ref name=autogenerated1 /> Her next film was ''[[The Flintstones in Viva Rock Vegas]]'', where she played the "ring toss girl".<ref name=autogenerated1 /> She also appeared in the independent film ''[[The Safety of Objects]]'' (2001), as the [[tomboy]] daughter of a troubled single mother ([[Patricia Clarkson]]). Stewart also played a tomboy, [[diabetic]] daughter of a divorced mother ([[Jodie Foster]]) in the thriller film ''[[Panic Room]]'' (2002), directed by [[David Fincher]]. She was nominated for a [[Young Artist Award]] for her performance.<ref name="Biography Today, p.158">"Biography Today", p.158</ref>

Following the success of ''Panic Room'', Stewart was cast in another thriller, ''[[Cold Creek Manor]]'' (2003), playing the daughter of [[Dennis Quaid]] and [[Sharon Stone]]'s characters. She was nominated again for a [[Young Artist Award]] for her performance.<ref name="Biography Today, p.158"/> About this time in her career, she started [[homeschooling]] because of her irregular schedule.

===2004–2007: Breakthrough===
[[File:Kristen Stewart Life Magazine 1.JPG|thumb|upright|Stewart in 2007]]

At age 14, Stewart's first starring role was in the children's action-comedy ''[[Catch That Kid]]'' (2004), opposite [[Max Thieriot]] and [[Corbin Bleu]]. That year, Stewart also played the role of Lila in the thriller, ''[[Undertow (2004 film)|Undertow]]'' (2004). Stewart starred in the [[Lifetime (TV network)|Lifetime]]/[[Showtime (TV network)|Showtime]] television film ''[[Speak (film)|Speak]]'' (2004), based on [[Speak (Anderson novel)|the novel by the same name]] by [[Laurie Halse Anderson]]. Stewart, aged 13 at the time of filming, played a high school freshman, Melinda Sordino, who nearly stops speaking after being [[rape]]d. Her performance was widely praised, with ''[[The New York Times]]'' stating "Ms. Stewart creates a convincing character full of pain and turmoil."<ref name="The New York Times">{{cite news|first=Neil|last=Genzlinger|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2005/09/05/arts/television/05genz.html?_r=0|title=For One Teenager, the Party's Over|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|publisher=[[New York Times Company]]|location=New York City|date=September 5, 2005|accessdate=June 13, 2014}}</ref>

Stewart appeared in the fantasy-adventure film ''[[Zathura: A Space Adventure]]'' (2005), playing the role of Lisa Budwing, the irresponsible older sister of two little boys. During their playing a board game, they turn their house into a spacecraft hurtling uncontrollably in outer space. The film was praised by critics, but Stewart's performance did not garner much media attention. Her character is immobilized during most of the film.<ref>{{cite news|first=Stephen|last=Holden|authorlink=Stephen Holden|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2005/11/11/movies/11zath.html?_r=1&oref=slogin|title=From Suburbia and Stranded Somewhere Near Saturn|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|publisher=[[New York Times Company]]|location=New York City|date=November 11, 2005|accessdate=June 13, 2014}}</ref> The following year, she played the character Maya in ''[[Fierce People (film)|Fierce People]]'' (2006), directed by [[Griffin Dunne]]. After that film, she received the lead role of Jess Solomon in the [[supernatural]] thriller film ''[[The Messengers (film)|The Messengers]]''.

Stewart appeared as teenager Lucy Hardwicke in ''[[In the Land of Women]]'' (2007), a romantic drama starring [[Meg Ryan]] and [[Adam Brody]]. The film, as well as Stewart's performance, received mixed reviews. That same year, Stewart had a small role in the [[Sean Penn]]-directed adaptation ''[[Into the Wild (film)|Into the Wild]]''. For her portrayal of Tracy&nbsp;– a teenage singer who has a crush on young adventurer [[Christopher McCandless]] (played by [[Emile Hirsch]])&nbsp;– Stewart received mixed reviews. While [[Salon.com]] considered her work a "sturdy, sensitive performance", and the ''[[Chicago Tribune]]'' noted that she did "vividly well with a sketch of a role", ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]''{{'}}s critic Dennis Harvey said, "It's unclear whether Stewart means to be playing [[hippie]]-chick Tracy as vapid, or whether it just comes off that way."<ref>{{cite web|first=Stephanie|last=Zacharek|url=http://www.salon.com/2007/09/21/wild/|title=Into the Wild|website=[[Salon.com]]|publisher=[[Salon Media Group]]|location=San Francisco, California|date=September 21, 2007|accessdate=June 13, 2014}}</ref><ref>[http://chicago.metromix.com/movies/movie_review/movie-review-into-the/209126/content "Movie review: 'Into the Wild'"], ''[[Chicago Tribune]]'' {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071221110856/http://chicago.metromix.com/movies/movie_review/movie-review-into-the/209126/content |date=December 21, 2007 }}</ref><ref>Harvey, Dennis. [https://variety.com/2007/film/reviews/into-the-wild-4-1200556779/ Into the Wild review], ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'', September 1, 2007. Accessed June 13, 2014.</ref> The film received [[Screen Actors Guild]] nomination for [[Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture|Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture]]. After ''Into the Wild'', Stewart had a cameo appearance in ''[[Jumper (2008 film)|Jumper]]'' and also appeared in ''[[What Just Happened (2008 film)|What Just Happened]]'', which was released in October 2008.<ref>[http://goldderby.latimes.com/awards_goldderby/what_just_happened/ Can 'What Just Happened' put Oscar champs Robert De Niro and Barry Levinson back in the derby?], ''Los Angeles Times'', August 8, 2008, Retrieved July 30, 2012</ref> She co-starred in ''[[The Cake Eaters]],'' an independent film that has been screened only at film festivals. Both the film and Stewart's performance received many positive reviews.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/the-cake-eaters/ |title=The Cake Eaters |website=Rotten Tomatoes |accessdate=February 22, 2015}}</ref> Critic Bill Goodykoontz from ''[[The Arizona Republic]]'' said that Stewart "really shines.... She excels at both aspects of the performance, giving Georgia a strength that defies any sort of pity one might feel for her, without letting us forget her vulnerability".<ref>{{cite news|first=Bill|last=Goodykoontz|url=https://www.azcentral.com/thingstodo/movies/articles/2009/03/13/20090313cake0313.html|title=The Cake Eaters|newspaper=[[The Arizona Republic]]|publisher=[[Gannett Company]]|location=Phoenix, Arizona|date=March 9, 2009|accessdate=September 14, 2013}}</ref>

===2008–2012: ''The Twilight Saga'' and worldwide recognition===
[[File:Taylor Lautner, Robert Pattinson & Kristen Stewart (7585905304).jpg|thumb|right|200px|Stewart with [[Taylor Lautner]] (''left'') and [[Robert Pattinson]] (''middle'') at the 2012 [[San Diego Comic-Con]]]]
On November 16, 2007, [[Summit Entertainment]] announced that Stewart would play main character [[Bella Swan|Isabella "Bella" Swan]] in the film ''[[Twilight (2008 film)|Twilight]]'', based on [[Stephenie Meyer]]'s bestselling [[vampire]] romance [[Twilight (novel)|novel of the same name]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.stepheniemeyer.com/twilight_movie.html |title=Twilight Series &#124; Twilight &#124; Twilight the Movie |publisher=StephenieMeyer.com |accessdate=January 2, 2010}}</ref> Stewart was on the set of ''[[Adventureland (film)|Adventureland]]'' when director [[Catherine Hardwicke]] visited her for an informal screen test, which "captivated" the director.<ref>{{cite magazine | first=Nicole|last=Sperling | url=https://ew.com/article/2008/07/16/twilight-hits-hollywood/| title='Twilight' hits Hollywood| date=July 10, 2008 | magazine=[[Entertainment Weekly]] |accessdate=July 26, 2008}}</ref> She starred alongside [[Robert Pattinson]], who plays [[Edward Cullen]], her [[vampire]] boyfriend. The film began production in February 2008 and finished filming in May 2008.

''Twilight'' was released domestically on November 21, 2008.<ref>{{cite press_release | url=https://www.reuters.com/article/2008/08/15/idUS216065+15-Aug-2008+PRN20080815 | title=Summit Entertainment Moves Up Release Date for TWILIGHT to November 21, 2008 | publisher=PRNewswire | date=August 15, 2008 | accessdate=July 30, 2012 | url-status=dead | archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130302034445/http://www.reuters.com/article/2008/08/15/idUS216065+15-Aug-2008+PRN20080815 | archivedate=March 2, 2013 | df=mdy-all }}</ref> Stewart's performance garnered mixed reviews with some critics describing her as "the ideal casting choice" and praising her for conveying "Bella's detachment, as well as her need to bust through it;" others criticized her acting for being "wooden" and lacking variety in her facial expressions, which they described as "blank".<ref>{{cite magazine|first=Owen|last=Gleiberman|authorlink=Owen Gleiberman|url=https://ew.com/article/2008/11/25/twilight-8/|title=Twilight|magazine=[[Entertainment Weekly]]|date=November 25, 2008|accessdate=June 27, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|first=Claudia|last=Puig|url=https://www.usatoday.com/life/movies/reviews/2008-11-19-twilight_N.htm?csp=IMDBreviews|title='Twilight' barely gets the blood flowing|newspaper=[[USA Today]]|date=November 20, 2008|accessdate=June 27, 2011}}</ref>

She was nominated four times, three consecutive years (2003–2005) and in 2008 for the Young Artist Award.

She received praise for her role in ''[[Adventureland (film)|Adventureland]]'' (2009), a [[comedy-drama]] film written and directed by [[Greg Mottola]], and co-starring [[Jesse Eisenberg]]. Critic [[James Berardinelli]] said, "Stewart is more than merely appealing in this role – she makes Em a fully realized woman, and some of the most intricate development results from what the camera observes in Stewart's eyes."<ref>{{cite web|first=James|last=Berardinelli|url=http://www.reelviews.net/php_review_template.php?identifier=1558|title=Adventureland|website=[[ReelViews]]|date=March 29, 2009|accessdate=September 12, 2013}}</ref> [[Kenneth Turan]] of the ''[[Los Angeles Times]]'' said Stewart was "beautiful, enigmatic and very experienced".<ref>{{cite news|first=Kenneth|last=Turan|authorlink=Kenneth Turan|url=https://articles.latimes.com/2009/apr/03/entertainment/et-adventureland3|title=Review: 'Adventureland'|newspaper=[[Los Angeles Times]]|publisher=[[Tronc]]|location=Los Angelews, California|date=April 3, 2009|accessdate=September 14, 2013}}</ref> and [[MSN Movies]]' James Rocchi stated, "Stewart's vulnerable, spooky power is used to nice effect." <ref>{{cite web|first=James|last=Rocchi|url=http://movies.msn.com/movies/movie-critic-reviews/adventureland/|title='Adventureland': A Game Worth Playing|website=[[MSN Movies]]|date=April 2, 2009|accessdate=September 12, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131005001636/http://movies.msn.com/movies/movie-critic-reviews/adventureland/|archive-date=October 5, 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> 

Stewart reappeared as Bella in the ''Twilight''{{'}}s sequel, ''[[The Twilight Saga: New Moon|New Moon]]'', once again receiving mixed reviews for her performance. Jordan Mintzer from ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'' called Stewart "the heart and soul of the film" and praised her for giving "both weight and depth to dialogue...she makes Bella's psychological wounds seem like the real deal."<ref>{{cite magazine|first=Jordan|last=Mintzer|url= https://variety.com/2009/film/markets-festivals/the-twilight-saga-new-moon-1200477394/|title= The Twilight Saga: New Moon|magazine=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|date=November 18, 2009|accessdate=June 27, 2011}}</ref> On the other hand, [[Manohla Dargis]] from ''[[The New York Times]]'' said Stewart's "lonely-girl blues soon grow wearisome,"<ref>{{cite news|first=Manohla|last=Dargis|authorlink=Manohla Dargis|title=Abstinence Makes the Heart…Oh, You Know|url=https://movies.nytimes.com/2009/11/20/movies/20twilightnewmoon.html |newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|date=November 20, 2009|accessdate=April 12, 2012}}</ref> and Bill Goodykoontz from ''[[The Arizona Republic]]'' stated "Stewart is a huge disappointment... She sucks the energy right out of the film".<ref>{{cite news|first=Bill|last=Goodykoontz|title=New Moon Review|url=https://www.azcentral.com/thingstodo/movies/articles/2009/11/18/20091118newmoon.html|newspaper=[[Arizona Republic]]|publisher=[[Gannett Company]]|location=Phoenix, Arizona|date=November 18, 2009|accessdate=April 12, 2012}}</ref> She reprised this role in the third Twilight film, ''[[The Twilight Saga: Eclipse|Eclipse]]''.

At the [[82nd Academy Awards]], Stewart and ''Twilight'' co-star [[Taylor Lautner]] presented a tribute in honor of the horror film genre.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.instyle.co.uk/award/page/23992/31718?destination=node/23992 |title=Kristen Stewart and Taylor Lautner team up at the Oscars &#124; InStyle UK |publisher=Instyle.co.uk |date=March 8, 2010 |accessdate=April 26, 2010}}</ref>

In 2009, Stewart starred in ''[[The Yellow Handkerchief (2010 film)|The Yellow Handkerchief]]'', which debuted at the Sundance Film Festival and was released into theaters in 2010 by [[Samuel Goldwyn Films]]. She also starred alongside [[James Gandolfini]] in ''[[Welcome to the Rileys]]'', which premiered at the [[Sundance Film Festival]] in January 2010.

In 2010, Stewart portrayed rock star [[Joan Jett]] in ''[[The Runaways (2010 film)|The Runaways]]'', a biographical film of [[The Runaways|the titular band]] from writer-director [[Floria Sigismondi]].<ref>{{cite news |first=Borys|last=Kit | title=Kristen Stewart to play Joan Jett | url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/kristen-stewart-play-joan-jett-123804 | magazine=[[The Hollywood Reporter]] | publisher=[[Eldridge Industries]]|location=Los Angeles, California|date=December 2, 2008 | accessdate=December 3, 2008}}</ref> Stewart met with Jett over the 2008–2009 New Year to prepare for the role; she prerecorded songs in a studio for the film.<ref>{{cite web|url = http://suicidegirls.com/interviews/Kristen%20Stewart:%20Jett-ing%20Through%20A%20Twilight%20Adventureland/|title = Kristen Stewart: Jett-ing Through A Twilight Adventureland|website=[[SuicideGirls.com]]|date = April 1, 2009|accessdate =April 1, 2009}}</ref> She received praise for her performance: the ''[[Metro Times]]'' wrote, "It turns out that Stewart is actually really good at capturing Jett's icy, tough-but-cool girl swagger, adding the needed touches of vulnerability that transform it into a pretty terrific performance... Stewart is a genuine rock star here."<ref>{{cite news|first=Bill|last=Holdship|url=http://www.metrotimes.com/screens/review.asp?rid=26192|title= Queens of noise|newspaper=[[Metro Times]]|publisher=[[Euclid Media Group]]|location=Detroit, Michigan|date=April 7, 2010|accessdate=June 13, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110714081816/http://www2.metrotimes.com/screens/review.asp?rid=26192 |archive-date=July 14, 2011 }}</ref> Also, [[A. O. Scott]] of ''[[The New York Times]]'' noted "Ms. Stewart, watchful and unassuming, gives the movie its spine and soul."<ref>{{cite news| first=A. O. | last=Scott|authorlink=A. O. Scott|url=https://movies.nytimes.com/2010/03/19/movies/19runaways.html?ref=movies |title=Proving Their Mettle in the Men-Only Era of Rock &#124; |newspaper=[[The New York Times]] |publisher=[[New York Times Company]]|location=New York City|date=March 19, 2010 |accessdate=May 11, 2010}}</ref> The same year, Stewart was listed as the highest-earning actress in ''[[Vanity Fair (magazine)|Vanity Fair]]'''s "2010's Top Hollywood Top Earners List", with an estimated earning of $28.5&nbsp;million for her film roles in 2010.<ref name="ibtimes1"/> She won the [[BAFTA Rising Star Award]] in 2010 and was awarded the [[Milano Film Festival|Milan International Film Festival Award for Best Actress]] in 2011 for ''[[Welcome to the Rileys]]'' (2010).

She was named "Stonette of the Year" by ''[[High Times]]'' in 2010.

[[File:Kristen Stewart, Breaking Dawn Part 2, London, 2012 (crop).jpg|thumb|upright|left|Stewart at the UK premiere of ''[[The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn - Part 2]]'' in November 2012]]

The fourth ''Twilight'' film, ''[[The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn&nbsp;– Part 1|Breaking Dawn – Part 1]]'', was released on November 18, 2011. Despite the film receiving mixed to negative reviews, Stewart's performance was praised. She concluded the role of Bella Swan in the final installment in the series, ''[[The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn&nbsp;– Part 2|Breaking Dawn – Part 2]],'' in November 2012. The film, despite mixed critical reception, was a box office success, grossing nearly $830 million worldwide, becoming the [[List of highest-grossing films|40th highest-grossing film]],<ref name="boxoffice.com">{{cite web|author=Boxoffice Media, Llc |url=http://www.boxoffice.com/statistics/movies/the-twilight-saga-breaking-dawn-part-2-2012 |title=BoxOffice® — The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn - Part 2 |publisher=Boxoffice.com |date=November 16, 2012 |accessdate=February 22, 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140811145712/http://www.boxoffice.com/statistics/movies/the-twilight-saga-breaking-dawn-part-2-2012 |archivedate=August 11, 2014 |df= }}</ref> and the highest-grossing film of the [[The Twilight Saga (film series)|''Twilight'' series]]. On [[Rotten Tomatoes]], the film currently holds a 48% rotten rating, based on 174 reviews with the consensus stating: "It is the most enjoyable chapter in ''The Twilight Saga'', but that's not enough to make ''Breaking Dawn Part 2'' worth watching for filmgoers who don't already count themselves among the franchise converts."<ref name="rottentomatoes.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/the_twilight_saga_breaking_dawn_part_2/|title=The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn Part 2 (2012)|website=[[Rotten Tomatoes]]|publisher=[[Fandango Media]]|location=San Francisco, California|accessdate=January 18, 2013}}</ref>

===2019–present: Return to mainstream films===
Stewart returned to mainstream Hollywood with starring roles in the action comedy film ''[[Charlie's Angels (2019 film)|Charlie's Angels]]'' (2019) and the science fiction film ''[[Underwater (film)|Underwater]]'' (2020). Both films were poorly-reviewed by critics, but her performance in the former was well-received.<ref>{{Cite web |title= Charlie's Angels (2019) |url= https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/charlies_angels_2019/ |website=[[Rotten Tomatoes]] |publisher= [[Fandango Media]] |accessdate=January 4, 2020 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/underwater_2020/ |title=Underwater (2020) |website=[[Rotten Tomatoes]] |publisher=[[Fandango (company)|Fandango]] |accessdate=January 8, 2020}}</ref>

Stewart will next star in ''[[Happiest Season]]'', a LGBT holiday romance film opposite [[Mackenzie Davis]] directed by [[Clea DuVall]] set for a November 25, 2020, release.<ref>{{cite web|first=Haleigh|last=Foutch|url=https://collider.com/kristen-stewart-to-star-in-lgbtq-holiday-romance-happiest-season/|title=Kristen Stewart to Star in LGBTQ Holiday Romance 'Happiest Season'|website=[[Collider (website)|Collider]]|date=November 27, 2018|accessdate=May 12, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/uncharted-movie-release-date-pushed-back-3-months-1272805|title=Tom Holland's 'Uncharted' Moves Back 3 Months to 2021|website=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|first=Aaron|last=Couch|date=January 24, 2020|accessdate=March 5, 2020}}</ref> She is working on her feature film directorial debut, which will be an adaptation of writer [[Lidia Yuknavitch]]'s memoir ''The Chronology of Water'', which she will also write.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.festival-cannes.com/en/festival/actualites/articles/close-up-with-kristen-stewart-feature-films-jury-member |title = Close-up with Kristen Stewart, Feature Films Jury member|date = May 15, 2018}}</ref> She is also attached to star alongside [[Riley Keough]] in a [[Netflix]] television series adaptation of Gina Frangello's novel ''[[A Life in Men]]'', produced by [[Charlize Theron]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.spoilertv.com/2018/08/a-life-in-men-charlize-theron.html|title=A Life in Men - Charlize Theron Developing TV Series for Netflix Based on Novel Starring Kristen Stewart and Riley Keough |website=spoilertv.com|date=August 9, 2018|accessdate=May 12, 2019}}</ref> and to play [[Joan Vollmer]], the most prominent female member of the [[Beat Generation]], alongside [[Ben Foster]] in his untitled directorial debut, an adaptation of [[William S. Burroughs]]' early works and letters.<ref>{{cite magazine|first=Etan|last=Vlessing|title=Kristen Stewart, Tom Glynn-Carney to Star in Ben Foster's Directorial Debut|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/kristen-stewart-tom-glynn-carney-star-ben-fosters-directorial-debut-1209173|magazine=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|publisher=[[Eldridhe Industries]]|location=Los Angeles, California|date=May 9, 2019|accessdate=May 12, 2019}}</ref>

On June 17, 2020, she was cast as [[Princess Diana]] in [[Pablo Larraín]]'s upcoming film, ''Spencer'', which focuses on Diana's decision to divorce Prince Charles.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Jr|first=Mike Fleming|last2=Jr|first2=Mike Fleming|date=2020-06-17|title=Pablo Larraín To Direct Kristen Stewart As Princess Diana In ‘Spencer’, On When Lady Di Rejected The Fairy Tale Ending: Virtual Cannes Hot Package|url=https://deadline.com/2020/06/kristen-stewart-princess-diana-pablo-larrain-film-spencer-diana-rejects-fairy-tale-ending-1202961395/|access-date=2020-06-23|website=Deadline|language=en}}</ref>

The series was released on June 30, 2020 on the [[Netflix]] platform.<ref>https://variety.com/2020/film/global/kristen-stewart-pablo-larrain-maggie-gyllenhaal-paolo-sorrentino-netflix-homemade-1234646217/</ref>
